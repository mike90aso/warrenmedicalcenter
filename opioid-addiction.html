Opioid Addiction Care with Dignity

If you or someone you care about is struggling with opioid use, you do not have to face it alone. Warren Medical Center offers compassionate, confidential primary care support for adults seeking help — with clear next steps and no judgment.

Schedule a Visit Call (313) 945-8210

A Primary Care Approach

Treatment for opioid use disorder often works best when it is integrated into ongoing medical care — private, respectful, and focused on your whole health.

Confidential & Respectful

Your visit is private. We treat addiction as a medical condition — not a moral failure — and we prioritize your dignity at every step.

Whole-Person Care

We look at your overall health, medications, mental well-being, and goals so support fits your real life — not a one-size-fits-all script.

Clear Next Steps

Call or schedule a visit. We will discuss options that may be appropriate for you and help you take the next step safely.

What Treatment Support Can Look Like

In a primary care setting, care for opioid use disorder may include evaluation, counseling about treatment options, medication-assisted treatment when clinically appropriate (such as buprenorphine-based therapy for eligible patients), monitoring, and coordination with counseling or higher levels of care when needed.

Not every patient needs the same plan. Suitability for medication-assisted treatment depends on a clinical assessment, your medical history, and shared decision-making. We do not guarantee a specific medication or outcome — we focus on safe, evidence-informed care and honest conversation.

What to Expect at Your Visit

  • A private conversation about your goals and concerns
  • Review of medical history, medications, and prior treatment (if any)
  • Discussion of options that may fit your situation
  • Planning for follow-up and ongoing support
  • Referrals to additional services when they would help

You Are Not Alone

Many adults in our community live with opioid dependence or are recovering from prescription or illicit opioid use. Seeking help is a sign of strength. Whether you are ready to start treatment, restart after a setback, or simply ask questions, our team is here to listen.

If you or someone else is in immediate danger from overdose, call 911. For the national Suicide & Crisis Lifeline, call or text 988.
