Immigration Medical Exams at Warren Medical Center

If you need a medical exam as part of an immigration application or related documentation, our primary care practice can help you prepare and complete the clinical portions of your visit. Requirements can vary by case type and change over time — we encourage patients to confirm current USCIS or attorney instructions before their appointment.

Who This Visit Is For

  • Adults needing an immigration-related medical exam or physical
  • Patients referred by an attorney, agency, or family member for medical documentation
  • Individuals seeking a clear, professional exam experience in Dearborn

What the Exam Typically Includes

Depending on your paperwork and current requirements, an immigration medical exam may include:

  • Review of medical history and current medications
  • Physical examination
  • Review of vaccination records and discussion of any needed immunizations
  • Screening questions related to communicable disease and general health
  • Completion of required medical forms to the extent our practice can support your case

Important: Immigration medical requirements are case-specific. Warren Medical Center does not replace legal advice. Please bring your instructions and confirm with our office that we can meet the documentation needs for your situation before you schedule.

How to Prepare / What to Bring

  • Government-issued photo ID
  • Any immigration medical forms or written instructions you have received
  • Vaccination records (bring originals or clear copies)
  • List of current medications and known medical conditions
  • Prior medical records or specialist notes relevant to chronic conditions
  • Payment method and insurance card (coverage varies; ask us in advance)
